    ‚≠ê Student Project Rubric¶

    This rubric provides comprehensive evaluation criteria for C-STEM student projects, including STEM fairs, project-based learning, and classroom projects. It integrates technical assessment with faith integration and 21st century skills.

    üìã Overview¶

    Purpose: Evaluate student C-STEM projects holistically

    Users: Teachers, STEM fair judges, students (self-assessment), peer reviewers

    Total Points: 100 points across five categories

    üìä Evaluation Categories¶

    Category Weight Description
    STEM Content & Process 30 points Technical knowledge, scientific method, engineering design
    Faith Integration 20 points Catholic teaching connection, purpose, and wonder
    Service & Application 15 points Real-world application, service orientation
    21st Century Skills 20 points Collaboration, communication, critical thinking, creativity
    Presentation & Documentation 15 points Display, explanation, documentation quality

    üî¨ Category 1: STEM Content & Process (30 points)¶

    Scientific Method / Engineering Design Process (15 points)¶

    Score Criteria
    15 Exemplary ‚Ä¢ Thorough application of scientific method or engineering design process
    ‚Ä¢ Clear hypothesis/design challenge with excellent rationale
    ‚Ä¢ Rigorous procedure/design iteration
    ‚Ä¢ Accurate data collection and analysis
    ‚Ä¢ Evidence-based conclusions
    12 Proficient ‚Ä¢ Good application of method/process
    ‚Ä¢ Clear hypothesis/challenge
    ‚Ä¢ Complete procedure/design steps
    ‚Ä¢ Adequate data and analysis
    ‚Ä¢ Reasonable conclusions
    9 Developing ‚Ä¢ Basic application of method/process
    ‚Ä¢ Hypothesis/challenge present but unclear
    ‚Ä¢ Some procedural gaps
    ‚Ä¢ Limited data or analysis
    ‚Ä¢ Conclusions loosely connected to evidence
    6 Beginning ‚Ä¢ Incomplete method/process
    ‚Ä¢ Weak hypothesis/challenge
    ‚Ä¢ Significant gaps in procedure
    ‚Ä¢ Minimal data
    ‚Ä¢ Conclusions not supported
    3 Insufficient ‚Ä¢ Method/process not followed
    ‚Ä¢ No clear hypothesis/challenge
    ‚Ä¢ Procedure unclear or missing
    ‚Ä¢ No meaningful data
    ‚Ä¢ No valid conclusions

    Technical Knowledge & Application (15 points)¶

    Score Criteria
    15 Exemplary ‚Ä¢ Deep understanding of STEM concepts
    ‚Ä¢ Sophisticated application of knowledge
    ‚Ä¢ Accurate use of technical vocabulary
    ‚Ä¢ Evidence of advanced learning beyond grade level
    ‚Ä¢ Clear mastery of relevant skills
    12 Proficient ‚Ä¢ Good understanding of concepts
    ‚Ä¢ Appropriate application
    ‚Ä¢ Correct technical vocabulary
    ‚Ä¢ Grade-level knowledge demonstrated
    ‚Ä¢ Skills effectively applied
    9 Developing ‚Ä¢ Basic understanding shown
    ‚Ä¢ Some application errors
    ‚Ä¢ Limited technical vocabulary
    ‚Ä¢ Some knowledge gaps
    ‚Ä¢ Skills partially applied
    6 Beginning ‚Ä¢ Weak understanding
    ‚Ä¢ Significant application errors
    ‚Ä¢ Incorrect vocabulary
    ‚Ä¢ Major knowledge gaps
    ‚Ä¢ Skills minimally demonstrated
    3 Insufficient ‚Ä¢ Lack of understanding
    ‚Ä¢ No effective application
    ‚Ä¢ No technical vocabulary
    ‚Ä¢ Concepts not grasped
    ‚Ä¢ Skills not evident

    üôè Category 2: Faith Integration (20 points)¶

    Catholic Teaching Connection (10 points)¶

    Score Criteria
    10 Exemplary ‚Ä¢ Deep, meaningful connection to Catholic teaching
    ‚Ä¢ Specific Catholic Social Teaching principle applied
    ‚Ä¢ Faith naturally woven throughout project
    ‚Ä¢ Student articulates faith-science harmony
    ‚Ä¢ Scripture or Church teaching referenced appropriately
    8 Proficient ‚Ä¢ Clear connection to Catholic teaching
    ‚Ä¢ CST principle identified
    ‚Ä¢ Faith integration evident
    ‚Ä¢ Student understands faith-science relationship
    ‚Ä¢ Some reference to Catholic sources
    6 Developing ‚Ä¢ Basic faith connection
    ‚Ä¢ General reference to Catholic values
    ‚Ä¢ Faith feels added rather than integrated
    ‚Ä¢ Limited understanding of harmony
    ‚Ä¢ No specific Catholic sources
    4 Beginning ‚Ä¢ Weak faith connection
    ‚Ä¢ Generic religious reference
    ‚Ä¢ Faith not meaningfully integrated
    ‚Ä¢ Faith-science connection unclear
    ‚Ä¢ No Catholic specificity
    2 Insufficient ‚Ä¢ No faith connection evident
    ‚Ä¢ Project could be purely secular
    ‚Ä¢ Missed opportunities for integration
    ‚Ä¢ No understanding of Catholic perspective

    Wonder and Purpose (10 points)¶

    Score Criteria
    10 Exemplary ‚Ä¢ Project inspired by wonder at God's creation
    ‚Ä¢ Clear sense of purpose beyond self-interest
    ‚Ä¢ Deep appreciation for beauty/complexity in nature
    ‚Ä¢ Student expresses awe and gratitude
    ‚Ä¢ Purpose connects to Catholic vocation
    8 Proficient ‚Ä¢ Wonder evident in project selection
    ‚Ä¢ Good sense of purpose
    ‚Ä¢ Appreciation for creation shown
    ‚Ä¢ Student shows curiosity and gratitude
    ‚Ä¢ Purpose includes serving others
    6 Developing ‚Ä¢ Some wonder present
    ‚Ä¢ Basic sense of purpose
    ‚Ä¢ Limited appreciation for creation
    ‚Ä¢ Curiosity somewhat evident
    ‚Ä¢ Purpose not clearly articulated
    4 Beginning ‚Ä¢ Little wonder evident
    ‚Ä¢ Weak sense of purpose
    ‚Ä¢ No appreciation for creation
    ‚Ä¢ Curiosity not apparent
    ‚Ä¢ Self-focused purpose
    2 Insufficient ‚Ä¢ No wonder or curiosity
    ‚Ä¢ No sense of purpose
    ‚Ä¢ Mechanical approach to project
    ‚Ä¢ No connection to creation
    ‚Ä¢ Purpose absent

    ‚ù§Ô∏è Category 3: Service & Application (15 points)¶

    Real-World Application (8 points)¶

    Score Criteria
    8 Exemplary ‚Ä¢ Project addresses genuine real-world problem
    ‚Ä¢ Application is practical and achievable
    ‚Ä¢ Clear beneficiaries identified
    ‚Ä¢ Solution is innovative and effective
    ‚Ä¢ Potential for actual implementation
    6 Proficient ‚Ä¢ Good real-world connection
    ‚Ä¢ Practical application evident
    ‚Ä¢ Beneficiaries identified
    ‚Ä¢ Solution is workable
    ‚Ä¢ Some implementation potential
    4 Developing ‚Ä¢ Some real-world connection
    ‚Ä¢ Application is theoretical
    ‚Ä¢ Beneficiaries vaguely identified
    ‚Ä¢ Solution needs refinement
    ‚Ä¢ Implementation unclear
    2 Beginning ‚Ä¢ Weak real-world connection
    ‚Ä¢ Application not practical
    ‚Ä¢ No beneficiaries identified
    ‚Ä¢ Solution not viable
    ‚Ä¢ No implementation path

    Service Orientation (7 points)¶

    Score Criteria
    7 Exemplary ‚Ä¢ Project explicitly designed to serve others
    ‚Ä¢ "Hands and Feet of Christ" approach evident
    ‚Ä¢ Serves marginalized or vulnerable populations
    ‚Ä¢ Student articulates service motivation
    ‚Ä¢ Connection to Catholic Social Teaching clear
    5 Proficient ‚Ä¢ Clear service component
    ‚Ä¢ Others will benefit from project
    ‚Ä¢ Service motivation evident
    ‚Ä¢ Student understands service connection
    ‚Ä¢ Some CST awareness
    3 Developing ‚Ä¢ Some service awareness
    ‚Ä¢ Potential to help others noted
    ‚Ä¢ Service not central to project
    ‚Ä¢ Limited articulation of service
    ‚Ä¢ CST connection weak
    1 Beginning ‚Ä¢ Little service orientation
    ‚Ä¢ Self-focused project
    ‚Ä¢ Helping others not considered
    ‚Ä¢ No service motivation
    ‚Ä¢ No CST connection

    üí° Category 4: 21st Century Skills (20 points)¶

    Critical Thinking & Problem-Solving (8 points)¶

    Score Criteria
    8 Exemplary ‚Ä¢ Exceptional problem analysis
    ‚Ä¢ Multiple solutions considered
    ‚Ä¢ Evidence-based decision making
    ‚Ä¢ Ethical considerations included
    ‚Ä¢ Deep reasoning demonstrated
    6 Proficient ‚Ä¢ Good problem analysis
    ‚Ä¢ Alternative solutions explored
    ‚Ä¢ Logical decision making
    ‚Ä¢ Some ethical awareness
    ‚Ä¢ Sound reasoning
    4 Developing ‚Ä¢ Basic problem analysis
    ‚Ä¢ Few alternatives considered
    ‚Ä¢ Decision making not fully explained
    ‚Ä¢ Limited ethical awareness
    ‚Ä¢ Some reasoning gaps
    2 Beginning ‚Ä¢ Weak problem analysis
    ‚Ä¢ No alternatives considered
    ‚Ä¢ Poor decision making
    ‚Ä¢ No ethical awareness
    ‚Ä¢ Reasoning unclear

    Collaboration & Communication (7 points)¶

    Score Criteria
    7 Exemplary ‚Ä¢ Excellent teamwork (if applicable)
    ‚Ä¢ Clear, articulate communication
    ‚Ä¢ Respectful engagement with others
    ‚Ä¢ Active listening demonstrated
    ‚Ä¢ Builds on others' ideas
    5 Proficient ‚Ä¢ Good teamwork
    ‚Ä¢ Clear communication
    ‚Ä¢ Respectful toward others
    ‚Ä¢ Listens to feedback
    ‚Ä¢ Works well with others
    3 Developing ‚Ä¢ Basic teamwork
    ‚Ä¢ Communication needs improvement
    ‚Ä¢ Mostly respectful
    ‚Ä¢ Limited listening
    ‚Ä¢ Some collaboration challenges
    1 Beginning ‚Ä¢ Poor teamwork
    ‚Ä¢ Unclear communication
    ‚Ä¢ Respect issues
    ‚Ä¢ Does not listen
    ‚Ä¢ Difficulty collaborating

    Creativity & Innovation (5 points)¶

    Score Criteria
    5 Exemplary ‚Ä¢ Highly original approach
    ‚Ä¢ Creative problem-solving
    ‚Ä¢ Innovative design/solution
    ‚Ä¢ Uses God-given gifts creatively
    ‚Ä¢ Takes appropriate risks
    4 Proficient ‚Ä¢ Good originality
    ‚Ä¢ Creative elements present
    ‚Ä¢ Some innovation
    ‚Ä¢ Gifts applied creatively
    ‚Ä¢ Willing to try new things
    3 Developing ‚Ä¢ Some originality
    ‚Ä¢ Limited creativity
    ‚Ä¢ Follows existing approaches
    ‚Ä¢ Creativity not fully utilized
    ‚Ä¢ Risk-averse
    2 Beginning ‚Ä¢ Little originality
    ‚Ä¢ Not creative
    ‚Ä¢ Copies others' work
    ‚Ä¢ Does not explore
    ‚Ä¢ Avoids risks
    1 Insufficient ‚Ä¢ No originality
    ‚Ä¢ No creativity evident
    ‚Ä¢ Plagiarism concerns
    ‚Ä¢ Does not engage creatively

    üìã Category 5: Presentation & Documentation (15 points)¶

    Display/Visual Presentation (8 points)¶

    Score Criteria
    8 Exemplary ‚Ä¢ Outstanding visual presentation
    ‚Ä¢ Well-organized and attractive
    ‚Ä¢ Easy to understand
    ‚Ä¢ Professional quality
    ‚Ä¢ Effectively communicates project
    6 Proficient ‚Ä¢ Good visual presentation
    ‚Ä¢ Organized and neat
    ‚Ä¢ Clear communication
    ‚Ä¢ Quality work
    ‚Ä¢ Project well-represented
    4 Developing ‚Ä¢ Adequate presentation
    ‚Ä¢ Some organization issues
    ‚Ä¢ Partially clear
    ‚Ä¢ Average quality
    ‚Ä¢ Project somewhat represented
    2 Beginning ‚Ä¢ Poor presentation
    ‚Ä¢ Disorganized
    ‚Ä¢ Unclear
    ‚Ä¢ Low quality
    ‚Ä¢ Project poorly represented

    Oral Explanation (7 points)¶

    Score Criteria
    7 Exemplary ‚Ä¢ Confident, articulate explanation
    ‚Ä¢ Deep understanding evident
    ‚Ä¢ Answers questions thoroughly
    ‚Ä¢ Connects all aspects (STEM, faith, service)
    ‚Ä¢ Engaging delivery
    5 Proficient ‚Ä¢ Clear explanation
    ‚Ä¢ Good understanding shown
    ‚Ä¢ Answers most questions
    ‚Ä¢ Makes connections
    ‚Ä¢ Adequate delivery
    3 Developing ‚Ä¢ Basic explanation
    ‚Ä¢ Some understanding gaps
    ‚Ä¢ Difficulty with questions
    ‚Ä¢ Limited connections
    ‚Ä¢ Nervous delivery
    1 Beginning ‚Ä¢ Poor explanation
    ‚Ä¢ Lack of understanding
    ‚Ä¢ Cannot answer questions
    ‚Ä¢ No connections made
    ‚Ä¢ Ineffective delivery
